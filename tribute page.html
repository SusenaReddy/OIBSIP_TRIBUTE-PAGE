               Sudha Murthy is an Indian author, social worker, and philanthropist. She was born on August 19, 1950, in Shiggaon, a small town in the Indian state of Karnataka.She completed her education in engineering and went on to become the first female engineer to be hired by Tata Motors. However, she soon realized that her true passion was writing, and she began writing books in both English and Kannada.Some of her notable writings include "Dollar Bahu," "Wise and Otherwise," "The Day I Stopped Drinking Milk," and "The Old Man and His God."In addition to her writing, Sudha Murthy is also known for her philanthropy and social work. She has founded the Infosys Foundation, which focuses on healthcare, education, and rural development initiatives in India.
